Exploiting Frequency and Spectral Clues for RGBN Spectral Crosstalk Elimination

Yuchen Wang, Xin Wang, Ziying Huang, Chang Chen, Ruikang Xu, Xue Hu, Yiren Zhou, Fenglong Song, Lizhi Wang, Hua Huang

Paper Code & Dataset

Abstract

RGBN cameras capture visible and near-infrared (NIR) information through a single sensor, providing additional spectral representation for scene perception. However, spectral crosstalk caused by removing the infrared cut-off filter severely degrades imaging quality. Existing methods employ end-to-end networks to directly fit RGBN data mappings, neglecting physical priors inherent in the spectral degradation process. In this paper, through frequency analysis and imaging mechanism investigation, we reveals two key physical clues for the first time: the frequency clue—crosstalk degradation primarily manifests as global spectral shifts in the low-frequency while high-frequency structural information remains relatively intact; the spectral clue—our analysis demonstrates that RGBN spectral crosstalk is fundamentally determined by the Camera Spectral Response (CSR), reflecting channel-specific sensitivity to NIR interference. Based on these clues, we propose a CSR-guided Frequency Mamba (CFMamba): frequency decoupling enables the model to focus on critical degradation components, while integrating camera spectral response as a key prior with Mamba's powerful global sequential modeling capability to precisely characterize crosstalk-induced degradation. In addition, we capture real hyperspectral data and simulate paired RGBN datasets across diverse scenes to evaluate the model under different environmental configurations. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing approaches on both synthetic and real-world datasets while maintaining low computational overhead.

Key Contributions

Two Physical Clues

We reveal the frequency clue (crosstalk mainly exists in low-frequency) and spectral clue (crosstalk is dominated by Camera Spectral Response) for RGBN crosstalk elimination.

CFMamba Framework

We propose a CSR-guided Frequency Mamba that uses wavelet frequency decoupling and physical prior injection to efficiently model global crosstalk degradation.

SDM Dataset

We build a high-quality hyperspectral reflectance dataset covering visible/NIR bands and generate physically realistic paired RGBN data for training and evaluation.

Figure 1: RGBN camera replaces one green channel with NIR filter, leading to spectral crosstalk in visible channels. Our method eliminates crosstalk via frequency and spectral clue modeling.

Method: CFMamba

Our CFMamba framework is guided by two physical priors: frequency clue and spectral clue.

  • Frequency Decoupling: Use DWT to split features into high-frequency (texture) and low-frequency (crosstalk).
  • High-Frequency Enhancement: Simple ResBlock preserves sharp details.
  • CSR-Guided Low-Frequency Mamba: Embed camera spectral response into Mamba for global degradation modeling.
  • CSR Modules: CSR-Embedding, CSR-Linear, CSR-GroupMamba, CSR-Channel Interaction.

Dataset: SDM (Simulated Diverse scenes Multi-spectral)

We construct a physically realistic RGBN dataset using real hyperspectral images and camera spectral response.

  • 530 hyperspectral reflectance images (indoor + outdoor)
  • 400–1000 nm wavelength, 512×512 resolution
  • 4 light sources: Sunlight, INC, Fluorescence, LED
  • Paired RGBN + clean RGB generated by real CSR curves
Figure 4: SDM dataset details: (a) power spectra of four light sources; (b) paired samples in diverse scenarios.
